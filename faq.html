Digestive Health

  • What type of diet is best for my digestive system?

    Eat a balanced diet rich in fiber, fruits, vegetables, and whole grains. Avoid excessive fatty, spicy, or processed foods to prevent issues like acid reflux and constipation.

  • Do I really need to drink water regularly for digestion?

    Yes. Drinking enough water helps digestive enzymes work efficiently, prevents constipation, and aids nutrient absorption.

  • What are early signs of digestive trouble?

    Common early signs include bloating, acid reflux, heartburn, abdominal pain, irregular bowel movements, and unexplained weight loss.

  • Why do I need digestive tests?

    Tests such as endoscopy, colonoscopy, and liver function tests help detect conditions like ulcers, infections, fatty liver, or colon polyps before complications develop.

  • How do treatments like endoscopic procedures work?

Liver & GI Health

  • What is a Gastroenterologist?

    A gastroenterologist is a specialist who diagnoses and treats diseases of the stomach, intestines, liver, pancreas, and gallbladder.

    WhatsApp Book Now

  • What is a Hepatobiliary or GI surgeon?

    A GI or hepatobiliary surgeon performs surgical treatments for liver, pancreas, gallbladder, and complex digestive tract conditions, including minimally invasive and transplant surgeries.

    WhatsApp Book Now

  • How does a liver function test work?

    Liver function tests measure enzymes, proteins, and substances in your blood to assess how well your liver is working.

  • Are digestive or liver problems hereditary?

    Some conditions like hepatitis, fatty liver, and certain GI cancers can have a genetic predisposition, but lifestyle plays a major role.

  • Do GI tests or procedures hurt?

GI Diagnosis

  • What is a diagnostic error in gastroenterology?

    Diagnostic errors occur when a GI condition is misdiagnosed, delayed, or missed, which can affect treatment outcomes.

    WhatsApp Book Now

  • How often does it happen in GI cases?

    Studies suggest 5–10% of digestive or liver cases may experience diagnostic errors, often due to complex or overlapping symptoms.

    WhatsApp Book Now

  • What causes GI diagnostic errors?

    Causes include atypical symptoms, incomplete patient history, lab or imaging errors, and delays in specialist consultation.

    WhatsApp Book Now

  • What are obstacles to an accurate GI diagnosis?

    Obstacles include overlapping symptoms, limited access to advanced imaging, patient non-disclosure of lifestyle habits, and rare conditions that mimic common diseases.

    WhatsApp Book Now

  • When is a formal diagnosis necessary?

    A formal diagnosis is required when treatment planning, surgery, lifestyle changes, or monitoring of chronic GI/liver conditions depends on an accurate identification of the disease.
